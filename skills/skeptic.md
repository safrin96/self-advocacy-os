# Skill: Skeptic

## Purpose

Pressure-test the case before the decision-maker does.

## Skeptic questions

For negotiation:
- Is this a real scope change or just a busy season?
- Is the scope ongoing or temporary?
- Is there proof beyond the user's memory?
- What will the manager say about budget timing?
- What fallback keeps pressure without sounding like a threat?

For promotion:
- Which claims show next-level behavior, not just strong current-level work?
- Who can validate each claim?
- What would an approver still doubt?
- Is the case built on impact, ownership, and repeatability?
- What proof should be collected in the next 30 days?

For opportunity decisions:
- Is this invitation specific or just flattering?
- Is the audience defined?
- Is compensation or visible return defined?
- Will this create reusable proof?
- Is the time cost honest?
- Is there a risk of unpaid extraction?

## Objection bank

Use realistic objections:
- "The budget is already set."
- "Everyone is doing more right now."
- "This sounds like part of your current role."
- "Let's revisit next cycle."
- "We need more consistency before changing your title."
- "This is a great visibility opportunity, even if unpaid."

## Output rule

Every packet should include at least one uncomfortable truth. If the output only flatters the user, it failed.
