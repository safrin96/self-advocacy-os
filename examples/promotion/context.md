# Situation Context

## Decision-maker

My manager, Senior PM. Reports to the Director of PMO.

She is supportive but stretched thin. She will back a promotion if I hand her a clear case she can present upward. She will not build the case for me.

She responds well to:
- Evidence organized by impact, not effort
- Clear "what changed" framing
- Names of people who can validate

She does not respond well to:
- Comparisons to other PMs
- Vague claims about "stepping up"
- Requests without a proposed timeline

## Workplace dynamics

- Tech company, ~1,200 employees
- PMO has 8 project managers. I am one of 3 at the PM II level.
- Promotion cycle happens in October. Cases need to be submitted by September 15.
- My title is PM II. I am targeting Senior PM.
- The unofficial expectation for Senior PM: cross-team program ownership, executive stakeholder management, and process contributions that scale beyond your own projects.
- I have been at PM II for 18 months.

## Timing

I want to have the initial conversation in August, before September case submission deadline.
