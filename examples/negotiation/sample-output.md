## Missing Evidence

- **Formal confirmation that expanded responsibilities are ongoing.** The senior analyst position has not been backfilled. If the manager considers this temporary coverage, the case weakens. Action: ask the manager in a 1:1 whether the expanded scope is expected to continue, and follow up with a summary email documenting the answer.
- **Written credit for the VP board summary.** The VP used it, but there is no paper trail connecting it to you. Action: send a brief follow-up to the VP referencing the summary ("following up on the migration impact brief I prepared for your board presentation") to create a written record.
- **Peer feedback in writing.** The peer comment ("I don't know how you managed three clients and your own projects") is currently a Slack message. Action: screenshot it or ask the peer if they would be willing to share that feedback with your manager.

## Primary Ask

"I would like to discuss a compensation adjustment and title change to Senior Analyst. Since taking on analytics ownership for both Marketing and Operations six months ago, I have been operating at senior scope: 12 reports across 2 departments, 3 direct stakeholders, and measurable outcomes including a 3.25-hour monthly time savings on reporting alone. I would like my title and compensation to reflect that scope."

## Fallback Ask

"If compensation cannot be adjusted before the August budget cycle, I would like three things: a written review date for September, documented senior-level responsibilities so the expanded scope is formally recognized, and a professional development budget for analytics certifications."

## What You Will Not Accept

Being told "we will revisit next year" with no written timeline or interim recognition of expanded scope.

## Likely Objections and Prepared Responses

**1. "The budget is already set for this year."**

"I understand budget timing is a constraint. That is why I am raising this now, before August decisions lock in. If a mid-cycle adjustment is not possible, I would like a written commitment for the September review and formal documentation of the senior-level responsibilities I am carrying."

**2. "This work may already be part of your current role."**

"My original scope was one department and five reports. I am now supporting two departments, owning twelve reports, and serving three department leads. That is a measurable expansion. I would like to make sure the title and compensation reflect the work as it exists today, not as it was scoped two years ago."

**3. "Everyone is doing more right now."**

"I appreciate that. My ask is specific to the scope change that happened when the senior analyst position was vacated and not backfilled. The reporting automation, client coverage, and dashboard build are documented outcomes with measurable impact. I am not asking for recognition of effort. I am asking for alignment between my title, compensation, and the scope I have been carrying for six months."
