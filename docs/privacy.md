# Privacy and Safety

## Your files stay on your machine

Claude Code runs locally. Your input files, evidence files, and outputs are stored in this project folder on your computer. They are not uploaded to a server, shared with anyone, or stored in the cloud unless you choose to do so.

## What Claude Code sees

When you run a workflow, Claude Code reads the files in this project folder to generate your prep packet. The content of your files is sent to Claude's servers for processing (the same way typing a message in Claude chat works), but it is not stored after the conversation ends.

## What to put in your files

Be honest and specific in your input files. The more real the evidence, the better the prep packet.

However, do not include:

- Passwords or security credentials
- Social Security numbers or government IDs
- Credit card or bank account numbers
- Medical records
- Other people's private information without their consent

You do not need any of these for self-advocacy preparation.

## Sanitizing evidence

If your evidence contains confidential company information:

- Replace client names with "Client A," "Client B"
- Replace internal project codenames with descriptions ("the data migration project")
- Remove dollar amounts if they are proprietary (keep percentages or ratios instead)
- Remove email addresses or phone numbers of other people

The workflow works just as well with sanitized evidence. The structure of the proof matters more than the specific names.

## Sharing your outputs

The prep packets in `outputs/` are yours. Share them with a mentor, coach, or trusted colleague if you want feedback on your case before the real conversation.

Do not share raw evidence files that contain other people's feedback without their permission.

## Deleting your data

To remove everything: delete this project folder. There is nothing else to clean up.
